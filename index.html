About Me

After graduating from the game design school Rubika Valenciennes, I finaly started my professional journey and now work as a game designer full time.

Through my students and professional projects, I learned a lot about games and especially how this medium can be used to tell powerful story through gameplay art and music making it an unique experience.

I personnaly thrive to learn more about combat design and world building and will one day make it my specialty.

I love drawing, it is both a part of my work and a great pleasure that allow to express myself. I regularly do sketches of system and mechanics I come up with for games giving additional insights.

I'm also very interested in the video game industry and like to stay up to date.

Anoter hobby of mine is watching and reading manga/anime/series, I'm very fond of a lot of japanese aesthetics that can be exploited in game to make them a go a level further.

Projects

Astral Ascent

I worked as a game designer and QA on Astral Ascent and was responsible for an array of tasks.

I started by a 6 months internship where I designed new system and mechanics for the game and helped the creative director in some of the design process and I handled playtest with the community to get more feedbacks.

Then at the end of this internship, I joined the team as a Game designer and QA fulltime and started working on additional task like programming in Construct 3, SFX and music integration, writing the monthly newsletter, ETC...

Fall Garden

Fall Garden was my last student project for my scholarship at Rubkika.

I worked on this project as the lead game designer with a team of 8 designers, programmers and game artists.

On this 5 months project I handled a lot of different task mainly making design documents for our programmer and art team but also help with programmation on Unity, level design, FX particules, SFX and music integration, project management, ETC...

Keep the beat

During this project, I worked as a game designer and game artist for this alternative controler project.

Keep the beat is a rythm game where the BPM is determined by your own heart rate. Fused with a unique controler and you get a fun experience!

This project was made with a team of game designers and industrial/objects designers wich proved challenging but very fun.
